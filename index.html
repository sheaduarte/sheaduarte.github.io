About Me

I'm a Ph.D. Candidate in Joy Geng’s lab at the UC Davis Center for Mind & Brain.

My doctoral research investigates how visual and multisensory perception interact with memory to guide naturalistic behavior. To study these mechanisms, I use psychophysics and virtual reality (VR) with integrated eye-tracking.

When I'm not in the lab, I spend my time in the ceramics studio, cooking and baking, and traveling as much as possible.
